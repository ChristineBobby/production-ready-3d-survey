Overview

Three-dimensional content generation has progressed from producing isolated, visually plausible shapes to constructing structured assets that can be deployed in real-time interactive environments. This trajectory is driven by converging demands from game development, embodied AI, world simulation, digital twins, and spatial computing, all of which require 3D content that goes beyond surface appearance to satisfy engine-level constraints on topology, UV parameterization, physically based materials, skeletal rigging, and physics-aware scene layout. Despite rapid advances in generative modeling, a persistent gap separates the outputs of current methods from the production-ready standard expected by interactive applications. This survey addresses that gap by organizing the literature around the asset production pipeline rather than algorithmic families. Along the horizontal axis we distinguish three asset tiers—namely general objects, characters, and scenes—while the vertical axis traces each tier through the full production lifecycle from data foundations and geometry synthesis through topology optimization, UV unwrapping, PBR appearance, rigging, and scene assembly. Through this two-dimensional taxonomy we assess not only what current methods can generate but whether their outputs are directly usable in downstream engines and simulation platforms. We further consolidate evaluation metrics and protocols that span geometric fidelity, appearance quality, asset usability, and scene-level physical plausibility. The survey concludes by identifying open challenges in data quality, generation controllability, end-to-end assetization, and physically grounded generation, and by situating production-ready 3D content as foundational infrastructure for emerging interactive world models and embodied intelligent systems.

General Objects follow a geometry–topology–appearance pipeline. Starting from multi-view or single-image inputs, methods first synthesize raw geometry (NeRF, 3D Gaussian Splatting, or direct mesh prediction), then optimize topology to produce manifold, quad-dominant meshes suitable for engine import. UV unwrapping and PBR material extraction complete the asset.

Representative Methods

  • TRELLIS.2 (2025) — 4B-parameter scalable LGM with PBR-ready output; sets new SotA on geometry fidelity
  • TripoSG (2025) — High-fidelity single-image to clean mesh reconstruction
  • SF3D (2024) — Feed-forward single-image to mesh+UV+PBR in under 1 second
  • MeshGPT (2024) — VQ-VAE mesh tokenization enabling autoregressive generation of artist-like topology
  • DeepMesh (2025) — RL-aligned mesh generation with clean quad-dominant topology
  • DreamGaussian (2024) — 3DGS-based generation with mesh extraction and texture refinement in 2 minutes

Characters & Avatars follow a body–head–rigging pipeline. Body generation methods produce articulated human meshes compatible with parametric models (SMPL/SMPL-X), while head-specific methods handle facial geometry, expression blendshapes, and hair modeling. The rigging stage assigns skeletal hierarchies and skinning weights (LBS-compatible).

Representative Methods

  • TADA! (2024) — Text-to-avatar on SMPL-X with disentangled geometry and texture; LBS-ready output
  • LHM (2025) — Single-image to animatable avatar with real-time rendering capability
  • LAM (2025) — Feed-forward head avatar at 280 FPS, mobile-deployment ready
  • GaussianAvatars (2024) — 3DGS bound to FLAME triangles; pioneering mesh-anchored Gaussian head animation
  • HRAvatar (2025) — High-resolution animatable avatar with PBR relighting
  • TexTalker (2025) — Speech-driven 3D talking head with texture disentanglement

Scenes & Environments follow a layout–grounding–world-scale pipeline. Layout generation methods produce semantic floor plans from text or image prompts. Grounding methods instantiate assets with physically plausible placement. World-scale methods extend to large outdoor environments, addressing LOD management, streaming, and physics-aware composition.

Representative Methods

  • Holodeck (2024) — LLM-driven embodied environment generation with interactive object placement
  • SceneCraft (2024) — Blender code agent that scripts full 3D scenes from text descriptions
  • Infinigen (2023) — Procedural generation of photorealistic natural environments at unlimited scale
  • LayerPano3D (2025) — Layered panoramic scene generation for immersive 3D environments
  • PhyScene (2024) — Physics-guided diffusion for interactable scenes with stability verification

Production-Ready Criteria

Six requirements a generated 3D asset must satisfy for deployment in interactive engines and simulation platforms.

Manifold Mesh Topology

Watertight, non-self-intersecting mesh with clean edge manifolds—suitable for Boolean operations, physics simulation, and engine import without repair.

UV Parameterization

Well-structured UV atlas with minimal distortion and no overlapping islands, enabling correct texture mapping and PBR material baking.

Disentangled PBR Materials

Separate physically based material maps (albedo, roughness, metallic, normal) that respond correctly to arbitrary lighting in real-time renderers.

Skeletal Rig & Skinning

A skeletal hierarchy with LBS-compatible skinning weights, allowing animation with standard mocap data or procedural animation systems.

Post-generation Editability

Generated assets can be further edited by artists in standard DCC tools (Blender, Maya, 3ds Max) without requiring full regeneration.

Physics Metadata

Collision meshes, mass properties, and physical material tags enabling participation in rigid-body or soft-body simulation within game engines.
